Art Form of the Month

Blue Pottery

Blue Pottery is a unique craft of Jaipur that traces its origins to Persian artisans. Unlike traditional Indian pottery, it doesn't use clay but rather a special dough made from quartz stone powder, powdered glass, multani mitti, borax, gum and water. The distinctive blue color comes from cobalt oxide, while other colors are derived from copper oxide (green) and manganese dioxide (black). The art form was brought to Jaipur in the early 19th century and has since become synonymous with Rajasthani craftsmanship. Each piece is hand-painted with intricate floral and geometric patterns, making them highly prized decorative items. The technique produces lightweight yet durable pottery that's both beautiful and functional.
