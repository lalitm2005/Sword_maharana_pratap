About The Sword

The sword of Maharana Pratap is one of the most iconic weapons in Indian history. Weighing approximately 25 kg and measuring about 5.5 feet in length, this massive khanda (double-edged straight sword) was wielded by the great Rajput warrior king in his battles against the Mughal empire.

This legendary sword symbolizes the indomitable spirit of Rajput resistance. Historical accounts suggest that Maharana Pratap could wield this enormous weapon with both hands, demonstrating his extraordinary strength and martial prowess.

The sword features a broad blade with a distinctive tip, a cruciform hilt, and often included a knuckle guard. The sheer size and weight of the weapon made it both a formidable combat tool and a psychological weapon against enemies.

Blade

The massive double-edged blade made of high-quality steel, measuring about 4 feet in length and weighing approximately 15 kg.

Hilt

The handle designed for two-handed grip, often wrapped in leather or cloth for better hold, with a weight of about 5 kg.

Pommel

The counterweight at the end of the hilt that balanced the heavy blade and could be used as a striking weapon.

Guard

The crossguard that protected the hands from opposing blades and could trap or deflect enemy weapons.

Tip

The pointed end of the blade designed for thrusting attacks against armored opponents.

History & Significance

16th Century Origins

Forged in the 16th century for Maharana Pratap, the sword became legendary during his resistance against Mughal emperor Akbar's expansion.

Battle of Haldighati (1576)

The sword saw its most famous use in this historic battle where Maharana Pratap fought against Man Singh I of Amber and the Mughal forces.

Symbol of Resistance

Became a symbol of Rajput valor and the fight for independence, inspiring generations of warriors.

Modern Legacy

Replicas of the sword are displayed in museums across Rajasthan and are central to cultural depictions of Maharana Pratap's legacy.
